How InboxCleaner Works

A complete A-to-Z guide on activating your license, connecting your account securely, and mastering the dashboard to reach Inbox Zero.

1

Installation & License Activation

Secure your local copy of the software.

  • Launch the App: Open the installed application to see the login screen.
  • Copy the Generated Key: A unique license key is generated specifically for your machine and displayed in the "Generated license key" box.
  • Activate: Copy that code, paste it into the "Enter license key" input field, and click Activate.
Once activated, the Connect to Gmail button will become available.
2

Connecting to Gmail

InboxCleaner uses IMAP to communicate with Google locally.

Security Note: You must use a Google App Password rather than your standard Gmail password. This ensures we never have access to your main account credentials.
Need help generating an App Password? Click to expand.

Google requires a unique 16-digit password for desktop applications. It takes 60 seconds to create:

  1. Go to your Google Account Security page.
  2. Scroll down to "How you sign in to Google" and ensure 2-Step Verification is turned ON.
  3. In the search bar at the very top of the Google settings page, type "App passwords" and click the matching result.
  4. Enter a name for the app (e.g., "InboxCleaner") and click Create.
  5. Google will display a 16-character password in a pop-up window.
  6. Copy this password (ignoring spaces) and paste it into the InboxCleaner login screen.
  • Email: Enter your full Gmail address.
  • Password: Enter your 16-character Google App Password.
  • Connection: Click Connect to Gmail. The app will verify your credentials and open your dashboard.
3

Using the Dashboard

Follow this A-to-Z process to organize your inbox:

Step A: Scanning the Inbox
  • Click the Scan Inbox button at the top right.
  • The app securely fetches the latest 1,000 emails.
  • The Progress Bar will show the status of the scan (e.g., "Scanning inbox... 44%").
  • Once completed, your emails are automatically categorized into Newsletters, Promotions, Receipts, Spam, or Other.
Step B: Analyzing and Filtering
  • Category Cards: Click on a card (e.g., "Promotions") to instantly filter the list to only show those emails.
  • Search: Use the search bar to find specific senders or keywords in the subject line.
  • Sender Analytics: View the Top Sender Analytics panel on the right to see who emails you the most.
Step C: Taking Bulk Action
  • Selection: Use the "Select All" checkbox or click individual checkboxes next to emails.
  • Archive/Delete: Click Archive Selected (moves to "[Gmail]/All Mail") or Delete Selected (moves to "[Gmail]/Trash").
  • Quick Clean: Use the Delete Promotions button to remove all identified promotional emails in one click.
Step D: Customizing the AI Classifier

If certain emails are being miscategorized, scroll to the Custom Detection Keywords panel:

  • Add specific words (e.g., "Receipt") to the appropriate category box.
  • Click Save Keywords and Rescan your inbox to apply the new local rules.
